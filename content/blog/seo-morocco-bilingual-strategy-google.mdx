## The Particularity of the Moroccan SEO Market

### A Market That Searches in Two Languages

The connected Moroccan consumer is naturally bilingual. When searching for a service, they might type:

- In French: *"web agency Rabat"*, *"website creation Morocco"*, *"web developer Casablanca"*
- In Arabic: *"إنشاء موقع إلكتروني بالمغرب"*, *"وكالة تسويق رقمي المغرب"*
- In romanized Darija: *"agence web f Maroc"*, *"site web b9al lfniyyin"*

If your SEO strategy only covers one language, you are invisible to **half of your target market**.

### Google.ma: A Search Engine with Its Own Logic

Google.ma shares the global Google algorithm, but with important local nuances:

- **Geolocation**: results favor businesses with a verified Moroccan address (optimized Google My Business)
- **Local signals**: citations in Moroccan directories (Yabiladi, Avito, Jumia) send local authority signals
- **Speed on local networks**: Google measures your site's performance under real network conditions in Morocco
- **Culturally relevant content**: content that resonates with the Moroccan context generates more engagement, which Google interprets as a positive signal

## The 5 Pillars of SEO Morocco According to EvoMarket

### Pillar 1 — Local Intent Keyword Research

**Keyword research** is the foundation of any SEO strategy. But for Morocco, you need to go beyond global volumes.

We categorize keywords into three intent levels:

**Informational Intent** (awareness):
- *"how to create a website in Morocco"*
- *"why a website for my business"*

**Commercial Intent** (evaluation):
- *"best web agency Morocco"*
- *"website creation price Casablanca"*
- *"web agency Rabat reviews"*

**Transactional Intent** (decision):
- *"website creation Morocco quote"*
- *"web agency Rabat contact"*
- *"custom web developer Morocco"*

A **Digital Sales Machine** targets all three levels simultaneously — attracting prospects at the start of their search and converting them when they're ready to buy.

### Pillar 2 — Bilingual FR/AR Architecture Without Technical Errors

This is where 95% of Moroccan sites fail. Managing bilingual content on a website is technically complex.

The most common mistakes:
- Using automatic Google Translate for Arabic (catastrophic for SEO)
- Not implementing `hreflang` tags correctly
- Having two versions of the site indexed without proper canonicalization
- Forgetting RTL (*Right-To-Left*) direction for Arabic, which affects rendering and user experience

Our approach:
```
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="fr-MA" href="https://yoursite.ma/fr/" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="ar-MA" href="https://yoursite.ma/ar/" />
<link rel="alternate" hreflang="x-default" href="https://yoursite.ma/" />
```

Every French page has its Arabic equivalent, translated by native writers, with researched Arabic keywords — not mechanical translation.

### Pillar 3 — Technical SEO: Speed as the #1 Factor

For Morocco, **speed is not an option — it's an obligation**.

Here are the *Core Web Vitals* we target for every site:
- **LCP** (Largest Contentful Paint): < 1.5 seconds
- **FID** (First Input Delay): < 50ms
- **CLS** (Cumulative Layout Shift): < 0.05

To achieve this on Moroccan 4G networks:
- **Next.js** with *Static Site Generation* (SSG) for static pages
- WebP + AVIF image compression with lazy loading
- Automatic *code splitting* to load only what's needed
- CDN with nodes geographically close to Morocco

### Pillar 4 — Reference Content for the Local Market

Google rewards **E-E-A-T** content: Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness. For the Moroccan market, this translates to:

- **Case studies** with real Moroccan businesses
- **Blog articles** that answer questions Moroccan entrepreneurs are asking
- **Practical guides** on Morocco-specific topics: *"How to integrate CMI on your e-commerce site"*, *"CNDP compliance for your website"*
- **Client testimonials** with names, Moroccan cities, and real photos

Each piece of content is built around a semantic cluster — a primary keyword surrounded by 8 to 15 secondary keywords covering the complete lexical field.

### Pillar 5 — Local Link Building: The Authority That Counts

Link building in Morocco works differently from European markets. The most powerful backlinks come from:

- Moroccan business directories: **Yabiladi Business**, **Maroc Annonces**, **Tout.ma**
- Moroccan media publications: **Medias24**, **Le360**, **Telquel**
- Moroccan tech blogs and influencers
- Chambers of commerce (CGEM, regional CCIs)
- Partnerships with complementary Moroccan businesses

We build this link network organically and sustainably — not with *black hat* techniques that risk getting you penalized.

## The SEO Mistakes That Sink Moroccan Sites

**Mistake #1 — Targeting too-generic keywords**
*"website"* or *"digital agency"* = impossible international competition. Target *"web agency Rabat"* or *"website creation Morocco SME"*.

**Mistake #2 — Ignoring Google My Business**
Your GMB listing is your first local result on Google.ma. Without it, you don't exist for *"web agency near me"* searches.

**Mistake #3 — Unmanaged FR/AR duplicate content**
Having the same content in two languages without *hreflang* = Google penalizes you for duplicate content.

**Mistake #4 — Neglecting mobile SEO**
73% of Moroccans browse on mobile. A non-mobile-optimized site = Google penalty + catastrophic user experience.

**Mistake #5 — No content strategy**
A website without a blog = a static site that Google rarely revisits. Regular content signals to Google that your site is active and relevant.
